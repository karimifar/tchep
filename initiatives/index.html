INITIATIVES

The primary vehicles for the activities of the Texas Collaborative for HIV Education and Prevention (TCHEP) are two learning collaboratives:

  • The Learning Collaborative for HIV Education will bring together community, state, academic, and community organizations, along with professional associations and other non-traditional entities, for collaborative learning about HIV education and prevention strategies among current and future medical providers
  • The Learning Collaborative for Best Practices in HIV Prevention will bring together health care systems, hospitals, and primary care facilities and providers to specifically discuss incorporation of HIV screening as a standard practice in emergency centers, urgent care centers, and primary care/community healthcare settings.

TCHEP will also conduct surveys of medical schools and providers to assess the current state of education, training, and screening around HIV in Texas, and will conduct an evaluation of reimbursement practices for HIV testing in Texas and the return on investment (ROI)/cost effectiveness of such screening.
