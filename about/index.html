About TCHEP

The Texas Collaborative for HIV Education and Prevention (TCHEP) is funded by the Texas Department of State Health Services, and supported and coordinated by The University of Texas Health Science Center at Tyler. An advisory committee of local and state experts and advocates will assist in steering the work of the collaborative.

The mission of the THLC is to advance widespread adoption of HIV screening, and other prevention interventions such as PrEP, PEP, and condom use as the standard of care in healthcare settings in Texas through policy changes and the education of current and future clinicians. This will be achieved through the collaboration of healthcare and community stakeholders in the development of joint initiatives, the advancement of best practices and prevention recommendations, and the promotion of education and training in order to:

  • Increase the number of healthcare systems and medical providers who adopt routine HIV screening as a standard of care to increase the number of persons who have been screened for HIV;
  • Increase the number of current and future medical providers who are educated about and adopt CDC and USPSTF HIV screening recommendations as standard preventive practice;
  • Increase the number of medical providers who are educated about HIV infection and transmission;
  • Reduce the number of undiagnosed HIV infections;
  • Promote early identification of HIV infection
  • Increase the proportion of persons living with HIV (PLWH) who are participating in HIV-related medical care;
  • Increase the number of PLWH who achieve viral suppression
  • Increase medical provider/patient education about PrEP/PEP and other HIV prevention interventions.
